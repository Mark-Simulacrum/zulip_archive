XAMPPRocky (Apr 10 2020 at 18:05):

Well I believe the motivation is a separate account ensures that you cannot cause catastrophic damage. This could be through a security breach on your end (stolen devices or information) or on a service providers end (such as admin capable API tokens being leaked). I do have a slight preference for separate accounts not from a security standpoint but an organization perspective. I've found that permissions are better when admins have to use the same permission structure as regular users for most of their work, because it makes any flaws or annoyances apparent to the people who can change it.
