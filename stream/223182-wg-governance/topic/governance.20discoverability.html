I like the idea. Rust's CONTRIBUTING is probably more helpful in this regard than most projects' would be; it links to the RFCs repository almost immediately after the table of contents. But otherwise, governance isn't really mentioned. There's a very short summary, mentioning the core team, in the README, but it's at the bottom of the document, in the "Trademark" section.

BatmanAoD (Kyle Strand) (Aug 31 2020 at 17:39):

It seems the blog post is suggesting that GOVERNANCE.md would be the "official" source of truth regarding how governance works; since Rust's governance is officially documented by RFC 1068, I'm not sure if the GOVERNANCE.md file (if we were to adopt one) would simply have a brief explanation with links to the RFC and to the "teams" page on the website, or if its content would largely be copied from the RFC itself.

BatmanAoD (Kyle Strand) (Aug 31 2020 at 17:41):

I actually think the latter makes more sense: that way, as new RFCs or the actions of this WG change how governance works, they would always be reflected by changes in GOVERNANCE.md. Updating that file would be how governance-related RFCs are "implemented".
