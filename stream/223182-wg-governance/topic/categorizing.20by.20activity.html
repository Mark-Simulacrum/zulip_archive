Topic: categorizing by activity

nikomatsakis (Jun 29 2020 at 19:51):

We had a really interesting discussion in a recent compiler-team meeting that kind of changed my perspective here. The core idea that @Wesley Wiser raised was that we could categorize groups not so much by whether they are pursuing a fixed project or not (as I had proposed) but more by their activity level. In the compiler team, we would have three levels of activity:

  • Notification groups -- no leadership or agenda, just a list of people to ping. We use these for things like "cleanup crew".
  • Project group -- has a lead and a plan and regular activity. It's something you can join. But joining basically means showing up and participating. If this activity slows down, may get demoted to a notification group.
  • Team -- an established project with a decision making structure. Membership is more of a commitment, and we really shouldn't let these dissolve lightly.

The meeting was held on Zulip, you can read it in #t-compiler/meetings > [design meeting] areas of the compiler compiler-team#288
