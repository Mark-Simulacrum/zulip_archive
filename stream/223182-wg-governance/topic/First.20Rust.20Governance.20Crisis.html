Topic: First Rust Governance Crisis

BatmanAoD (Kyle Strand) (Nov 22 2021 at 22:13):

The topic name comes from this Reddit post by Matthieu M written in the wake of his and the rest of the erstwhile moderation team's resignation.

While I have no further context on the instigating factors than what the moderation team offered in their resignation, I can't help but be reminded of this prior conversation we've had about whether or not the Core Team is in fact capable of "unilateral" decision-making.

Since then, of course, this group has become essentially defunct, for various reasons (despite the badge saying our status is "active" in the repo).

What I would like to know is, can this working group be revitalized to help address the current "crisis"? (And, for anyone with more insight into what's actually happening, do you agree with MM's assessment, which may be somewhat hyperbolic, that this is a "crisis"?) It seems like any analysis of the problem needs to happen in private for now, since the details of what transpired are intentionally not public. So presumably Zulip is not the right forum for that. But we do at least constitute a set of people interested in helping develop more transparent and resilient governance structures and practices.

@WG-governance
