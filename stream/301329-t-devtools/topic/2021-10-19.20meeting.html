Caleb Cartwright (Oct 19 2021 at 20:24):

AOB item

Completely unrelated to dev tools, but @GuillaumeGomez wanted to give you a shout out and thank you for your work in the gtk-rs space which I've found extremely useful (I assume that's you over there anyway :smile:).

One of the other projects I work on is Shields.io, which is the service that provides the majority of those status badges you see scattered across the internet. The badge requests we receive are overwhelmingly for SVGs but we also provide PNGs and have a separate microservice that handles rasterization.

We recently rewrote that rasterization microservice from a Node.js with Puppeteer & Chromium implementation to a Rust-based API that utilizes the bindings for Cairo, Gio and GLib and that change has been a massive win for us. It's provided a ~90% savings on hosting costs, and allows us to serve better badges (emoji support, letter spacing, etc.) with lower response times (and that's despite me lazily/clumsily stumbling my way through the implementation!)

Obviously all of those benefits don't necessarily come _directly_ from Rust, but the migration as a whole is what allowed us to realize those benefits, and those gtk-rs bindings were a crucial piece of being able to make that migration happen, so thank you!
