scottmcm (Aug 27 2020 at 17:47):

I think contributor can do this already without joining a team

Absolutely. You can change the compiler without being on compiler; you can change the library without being on libs or libs-impl; you can put review comments on PRs without having sign-off permissions; you can attend the language design meetings without being on lang; etc.

scottmcm (Aug 27 2020 at 17:52):

If you look at the "Welcome to the team" posts (such as https://internals.rust-lang.org/t/welcome-pauan-to-the-rust-and-webassembly-domain-working-group-core-team/9707?u=scottmcm https://internals.rust-lang.org/t/welcome-amanieu-to-the-libs-team/9269?u=scottmcm https://internals.rust-lang.org/t/please-welcome-nagisa-and-oli-obk-to-the-compiler-team/6712?u=scottmcm) you'll see that membership is generally extended in recognition of work that they've already been doing, not as as a prerequisite to doing that work in the first place.

Eric Huss (Aug 27 2020 at 21:12):

It is unfortunate that GitHub hides team membership if you are not an org member. Just to answer your question about determining who is on a team: GitHub teams are usually synced with the rust-lang team database. You can view it here: https://www.rust-lang.org/governance. Rustdoc is part of the dev-tools team (https://www.rust-lang.org/governance/teams/dev-tools).
