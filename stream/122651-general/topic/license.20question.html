scottmcm (Feb 28 2021 at 19:37):

Viliam Vadocz said:

I am taking inspiration from rust-analyzer.

What you need to do likely depends on the details of what "inspiration" means, specifically whether your language server is a derivative work of rust-analyzer.

If you started from the RA code and are incrementally updating it, then you'll need to follow the MIT/Apache license rules. But if you're using it as an example of how to speak LSP but writing everything independently, then you might not need do do anything -- ideas aren't subject to copyright. Of course, a note of thanks in the documentation is always a nice thing to do.

(Obligatory IANAL, etc.)
