Josh Triplett (Jan 24 2022 at 18:22):

Jacob Pratt said:

I'll ask though: what is the motivation? I don't particularly care either way, as I would either use 0x12345678 or 0xDEADBEEF myself. Just avoid some potentially sexist terms?

Right. Specifically, the kinds of things that can make people feel slightly less welcome when encountered. In the course of reviewing some of the network code, I came across these doctest examples (and the one above it), and started to prepare a PR to fix that. Then the thought crossed my mind that contributors to Rust might not know that this is an issue, and it'd be easy for tidy to catch these in the future, which would reduce the chances that a reviewer or moderator would have to explain it to someone submitting a PR in the future. (Such explanations typically involve some degree of "is today the day someone will pile on to my random GitHub comment, do I want to risk it", and I wanted to save others that in the future.)

Josh Triplett (Jan 24 2022 at 18:27):

(I was also inspired by the issue Microsoft had where they shipped Hyper-V with problematic constants as part of the kernel-to-hypervisor API, because they didn't catch that before shipping it. It was discovered later when someone found those constants in the Linux kernel.)

Josh Triplett (Jan 24 2022 at 18:28):

So I went through https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magic_number_(programming) and https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hexspeak , and added the subset of those that referenced humans.

Josh Triplett (Jan 24 2022 at 18:29):

And I guessed in advance that if people showed up complaining about it, someone would make the "0xDEADBEEF, what about cows?" "argument". I should have registered that prediction somewhere. :)
