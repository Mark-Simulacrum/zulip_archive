matklad (Feb 26 2021 at 09:48):

Responsibilities are a good question )

So far, as I was a de-facto lead, we weren't super strict about what it actually means to be the lead. I actually do think this is the right approach: if you have a closed set of responsibilites, you have a tendency to grind those responsibilities, instead of fixing everything that is broken.

So, yeah, I think wg-rls2.0-lead means the following:

  • dedicating significant amount of head space to thinking and caring about "creating the uncompromisingly perfect dev experience for Rust"
  • "transferring" the resulting system of values to the wider community of folks working on the project
  • having the "big picture" in mind
  • picking the "leftover" work and otherwise, fixing anything that's broken and isn't being fixed by somebody else.
  • and important bit of "leftover" work is coordination: changelogs, wg check-ins, etc

Notably, "understanding how every bit of code works" isn't on the list: I've never understood our type inference or proc macro expansion code, for example.
