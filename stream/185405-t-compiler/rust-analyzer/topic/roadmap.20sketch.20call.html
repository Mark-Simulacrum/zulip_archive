nikomatsakis (Feb 25 2019 at 20:45):

Hey all, so I had a thought after our meeting. First off, if I may summarize what I walked away with, it was basically this:

  • the "RLS 2.0" effort will define a kind of "subset of Rust code" (likely excluding some of the trickier macros) for which, in general, completions etc should work perfectly (this subset can be minimal but we should have plans to expand it)
  • in parallel, we'll be working on extracting and formalizing first name resolution (then type checking) into libaries shared between RLS 2.0 + rustc. These libraries would thus be fully correct.

nikomatsakis (Feb 25 2019 at 20:46):

My thought was that (a) I think this notion of a subset is pretty important in terms of public perception and (b) we should try to find examples of projects that fit into the subset at various points. e.g., if we can say "projects like X and Y", that not only makes things more real, it gives us great test cases to work with.
