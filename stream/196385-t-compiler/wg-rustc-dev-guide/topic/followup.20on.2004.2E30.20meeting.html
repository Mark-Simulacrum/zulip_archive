mark-i-m (May 06 2019 at 17:51):

Hi, I missed the meeting and just read the meeting notes topic... a lot of interesting ideas there!

Some thoughts I had:

  • I'm wary of adding a walkthrough/tour chapter without first discussing what the final organization of rustc-guide should look like. In particular, we already have "walkthrough of contributing to rustc" and "high level overview of the compiler" chapters.
  • I think part of what makes the rustc-guide feel disorganized is that there is no "flow". Each chapter was written by a different person, and there is no common narrative flowing through it. Moreover, each chapter is organized differently.
    • One solution to this might be to decide that each chapter will be organized the same way. e.g...
      • The top level chapter will explain the contents of whole chapter (like a detailed Table of Contents for that chapter)
      • The first sub-chapter should be a high-level overview of the technical content
      • All subsequent sub-chapters contain the actual technical content of the chapter
  • I think having a "here is the path your program takes through rustc" chapter is a great idea, as it helps put all the pieces into context.
