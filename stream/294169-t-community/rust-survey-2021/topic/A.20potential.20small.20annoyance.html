The "Where do you live" question has a very long list of answers, sorted alphabetically in English. However after translation, it's no longer ordered from translated text's point of view. It's potentially a little hard for user to find what they want in the translated list...

Xidorn Quan (Nov 06 2021 at 11:53):

To be honest, I think it's close to meaningless to have all the options in this question for many translations. e.g. majority of people using Simplified Chinese live in just a handful of countries (China, Singapore, Malaysia, and a small portion of expats in Japan, US, Canada, Australia, etc.), similar for Traditional Chinese, Japanese and many other languages.
It still makes sense for the original (English) version to list all, as English is the lingua franca where we should show inclusiveness. But it would have been better if translators can reorder and even hide irrelevant options for each language. That's probably some feedback we can give to the survey platform, though.

Xidorn Quan (Nov 07 2021 at 00:10):

From the answers last year, >99% of people filling the S.Chinese version of the form live in China, and all of those people have to scroll down the list to find China at 36th out of 195 countries listed there. I believe Japanese version likely has a similar ratio, yet Japan is even further down at 86th. Worse, people filling T.Chinese version most likely live in Taiwan and H.K., and they may have to go through the whole list of 195 countries carefully only to find their places not listed in it at all, given many of them are unwilling to have China represent their places.

Xidorn Quan (Nov 07 2021 at 00:21):

Letting people find from a long list without a quick access to what they need may just deter them from answering it, and some of them may also simply fill in the blank rather than picking from the list even if an answer exists there, which will add burden to translators rather than reduce it.
