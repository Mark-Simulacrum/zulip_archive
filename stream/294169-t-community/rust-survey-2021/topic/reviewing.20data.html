simulacrum (Feb 02 2022 at 20:55):

I think the perspective I have typically taken is that /everyone/ largely wants to make this more open, but doing so in a way that's more principled is probably best -- e.g., I would probably recommend we write up an RFC or similar style document that says 'who we share data with' for Rust project surveys -- the data is obviously valuable and useful. I suspect it's not entirely de-personal no matter what we do, so I would be hesitant to endorse a public sharing, especially after the fact. I think we largely promised to not share raw data, so to the extent we can keep that and iterate for future surveys, IMO we should.

To the extent Rust teams have interest in asking questions of the data, I imagine answering those by proxy is possible, though obviously not a complete solution.
