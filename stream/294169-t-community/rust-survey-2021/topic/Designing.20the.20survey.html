As an outsider, I'm curious: how do you decide how long to make the survey and what to prioritize? At least in organizational contexts, response rates drop off and ratings improve as survey length increases. Is that a concern?

rylev (Jul 23 2021 at 16:02):

I don't want to pretend that we have a strict methodology here. We'll just be talking through all the questions and making a judgement call if to include questions or not

Jasun Carr (Jul 23 2021 at 16:42):

From the previous couple of years, there hasn't been a noticable amount of response rate drop off due to survey length. Later questions in the survey move from ratings to rankings and discreet answer (eg. which of these tools do you use) which helps with some of the issues you raised.
As we look at adding to the survey and making adjustments, it is a concern that we will be keeping in mind (as well as potential order effects, redundancy, confusing wording, questions that are no longer relevant, and a couple other issues noted in the past).
