Jasun Carr (Oct 19 2021 at 15:00):

I'm taking a look now, and comparing the potential responses to the language/categories used in other publications (eg. NFS reports on Women, Minorities, and Persons with Disabilities in Science and Engineering)

What I'm seeing is mostly ok from a language/terminology standpoint - though there are potential area where the category is broad. Based on past surveys and connecting the multiple choice question to some of the short answer responses, the political beliefs option, for example, will encompass an exceptionally wide range - including those whose political beliefs are excluded from polite society. But given the global nature of the survey, many of these have to be left broad - and then can be drilled down into based on responses to the open-ended question that follows.

Jasun Carr (Oct 19 2021 at 15:03):

What I'm just now seeing as a larger issue is the structure of the question. It should really be broken out into 2: One asking if they see themselves as "a member of a group which is underrepresented or marginalized in technology?"; and one asking about the group itself.

Do you consider yourself a member of a group which is underrepresented or marginalized in technology?

Which of the following underrepresented of marginalized groups in technology do you consider yourself a part of?
