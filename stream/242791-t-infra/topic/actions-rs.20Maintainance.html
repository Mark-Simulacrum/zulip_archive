Brandon High (Mar 20 2022 at 01:41):

A couple of example from the actions-rs/cargo action:

  • There are 20 dependabot PRs open, 8 of which are security-related
  • It can't be configured to work on a subdirectory of the checkout, this PR adds a working-dir parameter to be able to to that. https://github.com/actions-rs/cargo/pull/59

actions-rs/toolchain

  • 19 dependabot PRs, 10 security related
  • rust-toolchain.toml support is missing, this PR adds it https://github.com/actions-rs/toolchain/pull/209

Most of the actions are in a similar state to those, with some community PRs to add missing necessary functionality and a lot of dependabot PRs. For the most part, the actions continue to work for now, so I don't think it's a critical risk but if things continue as they are, the community will continue to fork the actions off into their own fragmented namespaces, which I feel will make the experience of setting up CI for many new developers more confusing.

Re: svartalf's assent: I'm suggesting that it seems that the only party that svartalf would give assent to is someone from the rust-lang org because they seem to mistrust any community contributions. No worries if engaging with this conversation isn't something that the rust-lang org doesn't have time for, I just wanted to know if there was any feedback or input on how the community should proceed here because that will determine if I end up making my own fork or not.

Pietro Albini (Mar 20 2022 at 10:17):

In general the infra team is more focused towards the infrastructure of the Rust project itself, rather than providing infrastructure tooling for the wider community. We're also fairly stretched in the amount of time we have to work on things, so I'm wary of expanding the scope of the team.

Pietro Albini (Mar 20 2022 at 10:20):

Regarding trust, I fully understand and admire the stance of the maintainer of the actions-rs org: that organization is a huge attack vector (similar to extremely popular Rust crates), and if a malicious actor gets access to it they'd be able to compromise a lot of CIs. That's also the reason (for example) why sensitive repositories in the rust-lang org do not use any third-party action, and instead we hand-roll our own: I wouldn't trust random actions for example on rust-lang/rust.
