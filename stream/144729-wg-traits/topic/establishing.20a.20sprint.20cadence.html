Topic: establishing a sprint cadence

nikomatsakis (Mar 14 2019 at 16:08):

I've become quite intrigued by the idea of doing more organized sprints. Roughly speaking, the idea would be this:

  • Every 6 weeks, we have a special meeting (perhaps it is longer) in which we pick particular goals, which we should be able to connect to some of our larger themes.
    - why 6 weeks? Rust release cycle.
  • In between those meetings, we hold shorter sync meetings (like the ones we have now) in which we follow up on those goals.

The idea of the longer meeting is it includes time to review how things went and to decide if we want to continue pursuing the same set of goals, or something different.

We might consider doing the sprint meetings over Zoom, although I think I've found that Zulip can be surprisingly high bandwidth, and I know it's better for some folks (e.g., due to language barriers, bandwidth constraints, or other things). So I'd be game to stick with Zulip.

I feel like we already had a sort of informal "first sprint" meeting some weeks ago, can't recall the precise date. We could kind of "retcon" a sprint from that starting point. Or we could use this coming Monday to do a first sprint meeting.
