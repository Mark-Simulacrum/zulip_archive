nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2020 at 20:49):

rustc already has a notion of "implied bounds", but this would be expanding on that--

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2020 at 20:49):

today in rustc if you have trait Eq: PartialEq, say

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2020 at 20:49):

and then you say fn foo<T: Eq>(..), you also get to assume T: PartialEq

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2020 at 20:49):

so we expand "supertraits"

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2020 at 20:50):

but we have other implied bounds

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2020 at 20:50):

for example if you do fn foo<'a, T>(x: &'a T) { .. },

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2020 at 20:50):

then inside the body of foo, we have an "implied bound" that T: 'a --

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2020 at 20:50):

this is derived from the fact that the type &'a T is only "well-formed" if T: 'a

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2020 at 20:51):

but I've also been wondering about whether we can adjust the rules so that they match rustc more closely and approach implied bounds more as a "feature flag" sort of thing

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2020 at 20:52):

well, that chapter I think is a pretty decent explanation, but in short

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2020 at 20:52):

we have these FromEnv rules

Jack Huey (Mar 31 2020 at 20:52):

oh, this is new-style

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2020 at 20:52):

so e.g. if you have fn foo<T: Eq>(..) { .. }, we will add FromEnv(T: Eq) in the "environment"

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2020 at 20:52):

and then we have two other things

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2020 at 20:52):

first, we have a rule Implemented(T: Eq) :- FromEnv(T: Eq) --

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2020 at 20:53):

i.e., if you know that T: Eq from the environment, then you know that you can consider it implemented (Because your caller must have proved it)

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2020 at 20:53):

and we have a rule like FromEnv(T: PartialEq) :- FromEnv(T: Eq), derived from the trait declaration

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2020 at 20:53):

i.e., if the environment tells you that T: Eq, then it also tells you that T: PartialEq

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2020 at 20:53):

put those together, and you get that if your where clause says T: Eq, you also know T: PartialEq

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2020 at 20:54):
