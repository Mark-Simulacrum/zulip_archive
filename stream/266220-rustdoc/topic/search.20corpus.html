Topic: search corpus

jsha (Jan 04 2022 at 17:26):

To be able to test changes to search, I'd like to have a corpus of real-world searches that people have done on doc.rust-lang.org. When evaluating a change, we could run a bunch of those searches, and show how the results change.

One problem: most searches don't hit the server, so we have no logs.

However, _some_ searches hit the server. For instance, if someone has a search open in a tab, and they restart their browser, the server will see a request with a ?search= in it. We could collect a sample of those for analysis.

Thoughts? Would this be useful? If so I can go ask the infra team.

GuillaumeGomez (Jan 04 2022 at 18:39):

That could be useful, but the problem is to know if the results were useful to the user, no?

jsha (Jan 05 2022 at 02:11):

we won't know that, but we can see if the results match what we would expect, based on our understanding of the query.

But also diffing before/after results for a large set of queries can be an interesting way to understand a change better.
