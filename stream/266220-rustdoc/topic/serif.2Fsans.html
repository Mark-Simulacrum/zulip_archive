jsha (Nov 29 2021 at 00:06):

In https://github.com/rust-lang/rust/issues/90632 and https://github.com/rust-lang/rust/issues/59829, there were some requests to change to a sans serif body font for readability reasons.

I looked for citations on the claim that sans serif is more readable. It's a commonplace in graphic design, but not much supported by research. What studies there have been showed insignificant difference between the two.

So my default thought is to leave it alone, since change is likely to upset people and there's not a specific benefit.

_However_, I've been thinking about cross-site consistency in the Rust ecosystem lately, and I noticed crates.io, rust-lang.org, The Book, and The Reference all use sans serif fonts for body text. Being consistent with other Rust sites might be a good reason to switch.
