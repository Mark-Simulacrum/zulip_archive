jsha (Jan 12 2022 at 19:01):

so I actually have an idea for how to make themes lower-maintenance: CSS variables! https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/CSS/Using_CSS_custom_properties

You can see an example if you click through on the solarized branch I shared. You can also see a very nice example if you look at the CSS for https://www.powerlanguage.co.uk/wordle/ (ideally after playing a couple of words).

The idea is we would define semantic names like --foreground: black on the root element, and themes can override it with e.g. --foreground: white. So instead of having to override a bunch of rules, they override just a few variables.
