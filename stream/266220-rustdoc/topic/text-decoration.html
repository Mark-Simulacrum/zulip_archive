jsha (Dec 17 2021 at 06:17):

We handle text-decoration on links somewhat inconsistently:

  • Markdown headings (self-link): underline on hover
  • Rustdoc headings (self-link): none
  • Markdown hyperlinks: underline on hover
  • Top of page h1 (self-link and breadcrumbs): underline on hover
  • Jump to definition: underline on hover
  • Sidebar: highlight on hover
  • Links to items in a module (e.g.): none
  • Links to types (e.g. in method signature): none

Any thoughts on making these more consistent? I'm leaning towards consolidating on "underline on hover." That would mean changes to Rustdoc headings, sidebar, links to items in a module, and links to types.

jsha (Dec 17 2021 at 19:55):

Lately I've been using MDN as a guidepost on styling questions. I think they have really good design. I believe put a lot of effort into accessibility, and a lot of people like their site a lot.

Here's an example page: https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/CSS/CSS_Containment

Their approach seems very similar to what the Yale article says. For items where the linkiness is obvious from position (sidebar, breadcrumbs, headings), there's no default text-decoration, but they get underline on hover.

For links embedded in text, e.g. contain in the first paragraph article, there is an unconditional underline.

There's no equivalent in MDN of our "heading with multiple links in it," so no good comparison there.
