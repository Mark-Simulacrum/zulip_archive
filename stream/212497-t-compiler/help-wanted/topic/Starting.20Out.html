One thing that's nice when starting out is to just get used to the tooling. Run the various x.py commands. Maybe even make a silly change that does println()! every time a struct is created (or whatever). Then build the compiler! https://rustc-dev-guide.rust-lang.org/building/how-to-build-and-run.html

I love the rustc-dev-guide. In addition, reading through the rustdoc pages for rustc is invaluable: https://doc.rust-lang.org/nightly/nightly-rustc/rustc_middle/
