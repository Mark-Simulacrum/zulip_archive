bjorn3 (Mar 15 2020 at 20:09):

Hi Albert! If you want to learn more about how the compiler works, you can look at the rustc-dev-guide. If you want to fix an easy issue, take a look at the issies marked E-easy. If you comment @rustbot claim on an issue, that will let others know that you are working on that issue. If you want to work on the debuginfo generates for debuggers take a look at issues marked A-debuginfo. Most of these issues are not as easy to fix though. If you rather want to work on IDE integration, take a look at rust-analyzer. If you want to contribute to a crate about concurrency, take a look at the list at https://crates.io/categories/concurrency.

Whichever topic you choose to contribute too, good luck with it!

If you have any questions about rust-analyzer, you can post them at the t-compiler/wg-rls-2.0 stream. (If you have any question about the compiler internals, you can post it at t-compiler/help.)
