lqd (Mar 20 2022 at 08:42):

A couple thoughts:

  • the parallel compilation effort is somewhat stalled, and am not sure about its status. There is a meeting proposal so that t-compiler can discuss it, but it has not been yet selected for the next few rounds of meetings they'll be having because there are more important topics, that are technically related (as all this revolves around the query system, incremental compilation etc).

I've tried it quite recently and noticed that is stil compiles (it's tested on CI) but doesn't quite work as expected when actually turining parallel compilation on: there are a bunch of non-deterministic ICEs, of 2 types. One is related to incremental compilation (but IIRC they disappeared when turning that off), and the other is related to MIR stealing (and this would be orchestration/ordering concerns I'd say). So testing and analyzing this could be an interesting entry point although I'm not sure how hard that would be. I'm even less sure how hard it'd be to fix (I'm not participating in the parallel compilation effort myself, yet) and I know that the wg members usually have a lot of other things to do and not much time to devote to the effort.

lqd (Mar 20 2022 at 08:56):

  • compiler performance in general: the wg has a topic dedicated to ready to go tasks although I don't think we have talked about any directly related to "inter-crate sharing".

I'm not quite sure what you mean about that, and doesn't ring a bell, but we can talk about it in another topic there if that's of interest. I believe that we do have something a little bit related in spirit, which would be to improve pipelining so that more of the crate compilation work could be overlapped. That would improve both inter-crate parallel compilation (via cargo's parallelism, not rustc's) and build caching (a kind of inter-crate sharing but probably not as you thought?), by e.g. allowing overlap whenever there's codegen, by splitting linking from compiling. Work on this is very advanced, it's not clear to me how "done" it is yet. So it could require more work on rustc, and in cargo itself to coordinate these new phases.
