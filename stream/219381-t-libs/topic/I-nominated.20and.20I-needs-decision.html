Mara (Dec 30 2020 at 15:03):

@Léo Lanteri Thauvin I-nominated issues are to be discussed in a meeting, because they'd be stuck otherwise. I-needs-decision issues need a decision, but that doesn't necessarily need to happen in a meeting. For those we still have the hope that a conclusion can be reached on the issue/PR itself, probably with some input/discussion from libs team members.

I-needs-decision issues do appear in our triage meeting agenda though, but we discuss I-nominated issues first. I-needs-decision issues are only discussed in the meetings if there's time left.

pnkfelix (Feb 03 2021 at 14:59):

The differences in team protocols here is interesting to observe. T-compiler currently puts I-needs-decision stuff earlier in agenda than I-nominated stuff. Historically we had a hard time getting through all the I-nominated stuff and so putting it early would mean that nothing else got addressed. So eventually the I-nominated list was moved to the back. More recently we’ve been better about trying to get through it.
