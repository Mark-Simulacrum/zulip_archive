My understanding is that the previous discussion had aimed to get it in front of people and get lots of usage feedback before a specific RFC was written and accepted.

the logic being that basically the RFC can say "we want to do this in general" and not specify any specific design/API details, or the RFC can come after people have kicked the tires in Nightly and then specify some or all of the design/API details, but what can't realistically happen for something this large is trying to RFC a particular design/API without solid experimentation on what's actually good to use.

Jubilee (Oct 05 2021 at 05:13):

Ah, yes. Specifically we were chartered in https://github.com/rust-lang/rfcs/pull/2977 for

  • Determine the shape of the portable SIMD API.
  • Get an unstable std::simd and core::simd API in the standard library. This may mean renaming packed_simd to stdsimd and working directly on it, or creating a new repository and pulling in chunks of code as needed.
  • Produce a stabilization plan to allow portions of std::simd to be stabilized when they're ready, and coordinate with other unstable features.
  • Respond to user feedback and review contributions to the API.
  • Update [RFC 2948] based on the final API and stabilization plan.
  • Stabilize std::simd!
