Nick Cameron (Feb 24 2022 at 10:28):

I would say it is worth also stating what the team thinks is important and would get done if it were possible. Even though people mostly work on what they want because open source, I think it if you can paint a compelling picture of how things could be, that can shape what people want to work on (especially for new contributors). It is also useful for people to prioritise their 'overhead' time, e.g., folk don't have enough time to review everything straight away and tend to do at least some review work on what they think is important in general, not just important for their goals, so being explicit about what the team thinks is important is useful there.
