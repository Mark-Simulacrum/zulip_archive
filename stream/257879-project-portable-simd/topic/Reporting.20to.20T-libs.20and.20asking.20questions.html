Josh Triplett (Jun 11 2021 at 17:53):

@Jubilee From a T-libs perspective, I don't think an RFC is even the ideal way to do a "check-in / design review"; an informal document and a conversation in a meeting seems preferable for that. And to get into nightly, a PR seems fine.

Josh Triplett (Jun 11 2021 at 17:54):

That said, starting to draft an RFC seems like a really good idea, and a draft of that RFC might form part of a document that helps drive a check-in / design review meeting.

Josh Triplett (Jun 11 2021 at 17:55):

But yeah, from a libs perspective, RFCs are for stabilization, nightly works via PRs and other documents and meetings.

Miguel Raz Guzmán Macedo (Jun 11 2021 at 18:07):

Thanks for the input @Josh Triplett - I'm a bit new to the RFC process. I'm guessing this is a good guide for RFC's
https://rust-lang.github.io/rfcs/#rust-rfcs

Josh Triplett (Jun 11 2021 at 18:09):

It is, but it's also worth noting that different teams use RFCs a little differently.

Josh Triplett (Jun 11 2021 at 18:10):

For instance, lang and compiler have the MCP process as a way to start a conversation and work effort, while libs an cargo don't have that (but we've thought about introducing it).

Josh Triplett (Jun 11 2021 at 18:10):

And lang tends to operate by "everything needs either an RFC or MCP", while libs doesn't mind doing nightly-only changes via PRs.
