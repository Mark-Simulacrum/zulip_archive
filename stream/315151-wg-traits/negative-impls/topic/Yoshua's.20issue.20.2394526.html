Daniel Henry-Mantilla (Mar 18 2022 at 00:20):

nikomatsakis said:

btw I'm not sure that !Future for ! makes sense

Since Future has an associated type, I'd rather we didn't have ! : Future, since for some arbitrary type T, we won't have ! : Future<Output = T> anyways: the only one slightly sensible choice
would be ! : Future<Output = !>, and I'm not convinced that we should favor ! in assoc type like that.
Another way to look at this, is that we want !Future for Result<!, _>, which thus includes !Future for Result<!, !>, and I don't see why ! would have to feature trait impls that Result<!, !> would not have.
