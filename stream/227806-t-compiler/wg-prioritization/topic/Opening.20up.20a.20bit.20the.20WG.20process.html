hello @WG-prioritization !

Yesterday I was reviewing the Compiler team agenda with Santiago, this is something that is going on since a couple of weeks - he's mentoring me every week so I can help him on this task (you will probably have noticed that I'm getting more and more active in this stream).

I think that we have good documentation of our workflow (thanks to those that took the time to write it down) but we also discussed that there are things that cannot be strictly defined in a bullet list. It's a kind of "tribal knowledge" that levels up the experience in doing anything. The kind of thing that forge the judgement skills.

So if on the one hand we kept these "operational chats" out of band to avoid polluting this stream, we also thought that all that is then lost. I am the only recipient of something that could be beneficial to others. Especially now that we are publishing a call for contributors, so I expect new faces to show up.

So, long story short, we want to find a way to keep more stuff in the open in a way that is not bothering anyone, but fully accessible and interactive at the same time. I think this will slightly add a bit of overhead, but we hope that it will have a positive outcome. In any case it's an experiment, so we'll see where it goes :-)

We are of course open to suggestions and comments, we are exploring this space all together.

My proposal: having one clearly recognizable topic in this stream that people can either mute, follow or jump in as they wish. In that stream Santiago and I will write whatever operational stuff that is not relevant to a) specific prioritization github issues (there's the substream /alerts for that) and b) things that deserve their own specific Zulip topic.

sorry for the long rant - I am bad at summarizing :)
