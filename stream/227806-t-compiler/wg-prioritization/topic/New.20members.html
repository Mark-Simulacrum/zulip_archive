DPC (Sep 15 2020 at 19:48):

to summarise, the general process is:

  • an issue gets marked by a triagist / any random person as I-prioritize, and a thread is automatically created in #t-compiler/wg-prioritization/alerts
  • WG members look at the issue and discuss what priority it deserves
  • after there is sufficient consensus (many people agree to the same priority or after waiting for roughly a day) the issue is marked with the corresponding priority label (p-*) with a note linking to the thread where it was discussed and I-prioritize is removed
  • P-critical / P-high issues make their way to the compiler team meetings which happens every Thursday (so you'll see more activity here on Wednesdays)

As newcomers to the group you might find it tricky to assess the priority, but free to voice your opinion about an issue :smile: (there's more on our process written down somewhere :stuck_out_tongue: )
