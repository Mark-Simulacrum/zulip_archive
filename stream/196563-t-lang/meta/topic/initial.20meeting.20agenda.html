Elahn Ientile (May 04 2019 at 00:35):

Regarding, "we need to be able to communicate when we have eliminated options from discussion – and not relitigate those questions." The reasons for elimination need to be clear in order to avoid relitigation, otherwise "aggressive moderation" will feel like ignoring valid feedback.

Providing references for those reasons would also be helpful, along with a statement for newcomers like: "if you think these reasons are invalid, please read the referenced discussion and see if your objections have been addressed previously. If not, we'd love to know why these reasons are invalid, so we can reopen the discussion." And we could link to a guide with some good examples of how to do this with more than subjective feelings.

This "gets people on the same page" and allows them to dig into why options were dismissed, without needing to read the entire original design discussion(s).
