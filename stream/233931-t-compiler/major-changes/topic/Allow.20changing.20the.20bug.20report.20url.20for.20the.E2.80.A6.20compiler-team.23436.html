Josh Triplett (Jun 30 2021 at 03:55):

It might make sense to provide some guidance on whether we expect Linux distributions and similar to change this. Sometimes, when a distribution like Debian sees a configurable bug-reporting URL, they change it to their distribution bug tracker, on the theory that they should be filtering bugs before sending them upstream (in case they're distribution-specific or caused by a distribution change). We should decide if we want that to happen or not, and provide some documentation right next to the setting accordingly.

Joshua Nelson (Jun 30 2021 at 03:55):

They were already able to do that by patching the hard-coded URL. I don't see why allowing it to change at runtime would change things.

Josh Triplett (Jun 30 2021 at 04:27):

@Joshua Nelson Because it offers a clear knob to tweak, and distributions look for those knobs.

Josh Triplett (Jun 30 2021 at 04:28):

I'm suggesting that in the spot where we document that knob, we document our expectations. Is this only for tools that reuse internal bits of rustc? Or is this also for distributors, who might think they should be taking more responsibility for bug reports?
