Daniel Henry-Mantilla (Nov 08 2021 at 14:12):

To add to @Sean Chen (he/him)'s comment: I think that given the amount of people interested here, it would really be a pity if such a reading club were to go unrecorded. Recording the presentation would have many advantages, and would only have one non-negligible drawback: deterring shy people from asking questions. I don't think that the latter can be as bad as gatekeeping, if only by the fact of being available exactly at the time of the reading, or even by other reasons should there be too many people interested, as seems to be the case.

Do note that deterring shy people from asking questions is not to be underestimated or dismissed —I'm not advocating for that at all!—, and so is definitely something that should at least be discussed / thought about. My personal idea here (besides the fact that a meeting with 100+ people live can be quite deterring as well), would be that the person(s) making the presentation (@nikomatsakis?) record their screen & their mics, but not the rest of the (meeting's) audio. Ideally, the people making the presentation / the reading would remember to repeat the question (but if they forget it wouldn't be that bad either: I'd rather miss an attendee's question on the recording than the whole recording altogether!).

On top of the "this info will remain available to future people interested on the topic or those unable to attend it", there is also another benefit for the meeting itself. I know that if it were to be recorded, for instance, I'd gladly not attend the live / real-time presentation, and from there it's fair to assume that some (how many?) other people would feel the same, which could relieve some of the logistical pressure of the meeting :smile:

And if any of you were to be willing to "repeat reading sessions", they could then have some of the following reading sessions go unrecorded, so as to not totally lose the unrecorded talk opportunity if it's deemed desirable or valuable enough.

If only, a poll regarding what others think on the subject could yield interesting results.
