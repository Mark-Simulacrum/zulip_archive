inquisitivecrystal (Jan 21 2022 at 08:45):

pnkfelix said:

In some ways, my attempt to schedule this meeting is me assuming that 1. the definitions in that forge link are accurate and 2. the P-labels are semi-accurate

I'll post some thoughts I have (purely my own perspective, of course). It might also be worthwhile bringing in more of wg-prioritization for discussions about this. I have the highest confidence in apiraino, our lead, more perspectives can hardly hurt.

Generally, each prioritization is commented on by multiple members of the WG. The prioritization that results is a consensus of those that comment. However, we don't really... look at a criteria sheet? I'm pretty sure I've read that at some point in the past, but I don't know the last time I looked at it personally. In practice, discussions are more subjective than that, so one thing a few members have been concerned about is that we could be vulnerable to drift over time.

That being said, I think our prioritization are generally reasonable, and that our use of P-critical more or less aligns with those criteria, though we are probably a bit more restrictive. For instance, an ICE on invalid code is never assigned a P-critical, even if there's no meaningful error. #91206, which is about the most common imaginable such ICE, only got P-high. P-medium would be the norm.

There are also a bunch of factors we consider that go beyond what's on that sheet. For instance, that classification doesn't describe how to deal with performance regressions. The way a performance regression needs to be handled depends greatly on the size of the regression. There are cases where we see regressions that are so bad the code may as well not compile at all, but it's not clear where the cutoff for that is. Additionally, for platform specific problems, we often consider the tier of the platform.

I'd personally kind of like to see a more comprehensive review where both T-compiler and WG-prioritization are involved, and issues of all prioritizations are sampled. Something explicitly focused on ensuring that prioritizations are accurate and useful across the board, beyond just assigning P-high issues. We could make sure that the criteria we use match up with what T-compiler wants, and then update the written documentation to match what we use in practice. That's just an idea I had though, and I in no way speak for the WG, nor have I previously discussed this this with anyone else on it.
