@Vadim Petrochenkov in https://github.com/rust-lang/rust/pull/93017#issuecomment-1019151425 you mention moving stability checks to the resolver. What would be the benefit of doing so?

Gary Guo (Jan 23 2022 at 17:24):

I assume it's related to path stability https://github.com/rust-lang/rust/issues/15702

Vadim Petrochenkov (Jan 24 2022 at 04:44):

When we resolving a name during name resolution we know which specific import introduced that name, so we can make stability and deprecation attributes work on reexports (there are multiple issues about this scattered across the bugtracker).

Vadim Petrochenkov (Jan 24 2022 at 04:47):

The issue with stability checking for individual path segments now have an alternative solution because we keep resolutions for individual segments now (or at least for some subset of them), but doing it during name resolution is also possible.

Vadim Petrochenkov (Jan 24 2022 at 04:49):

These are mostly internal issues though (except for perhaps #[deprecated] on reexports), so we can also declare them wontfix and do whatever is more convenient for the compiler (e.g. nothing).
