Topic: Hosting meetings agenda on a git repo

apiraino (Sep 16 2021 at 10:22):

hello team!

Since a while I was thinking about handling the T-compiler agenda workflow in a slightly different way.
Pain points: the HackMD web interface is really slow to use. Rarely happened but a lack of sync with their servers had me lose some content.

My suggestion is the following workflow:

  • Create a subdir on https://github.com/rust-lang/compiler-team/ (or any other repo under rust-lang that someone from WG-prioritization can have write perms) and push there all the past meetings agenda
  • Every week add a new document generated by our trusted triagebot
  • Offline edit the agenda with everyone's preferred tool
  • Paste the finalized .md document on HackMD for everyone to see during the meeting
  • (further edits and refinements during or after the meeting are manually synced back to the git repo)

Opinions?
