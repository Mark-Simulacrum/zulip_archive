nikomatsakis said:

(before ?Sized, we had types like [Foo]/~ for Box<[Foo]> and so forth)

How was [Foo]/~ different from ~[Foo]?

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2022 at 16:26):

it was not decomposable

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2022 at 16:26):

i.e., ~[Foo] (or Box<[Foo]> today) is actually composed of

  • T ::= ~T
  • T ::= [T]

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2022 at 16:26):

but the idea was T ::= [T]/~

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2022 at 16:27):

and [T] was not a type

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2022 at 16:27):

i.e., you baked in the kind-of-pointer + referent inseparably

eholk (Mar 31 2022 at 16:27):

Ah, so [T] was something like "a thing that can live behind a pointer"?

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2022 at 16:27):

similar in some sense to dyn*

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2022 at 16:27):

it just wasn't a thing at all

nikomatsakis (Mar 31 2022 at 16:27):

you couldn't, e.g., implement a trait for [T], because that was not a type
