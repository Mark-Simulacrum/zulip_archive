lqd (May 17 2021 at 16:30):

I'm not sure how less successful the shrinkmem sprint was compared to the polonius sprints ? it could be interesting to compare in what ways you thought the rustc one was quote/unquote unsuccessful (was it in the results, organization, participation, and so on ?)

I can tell they were different in a couple ways:

  • there were no themes for the polonius sprints, compared to say "shrinking memory usage"; of course it's understandable that a theme would be necessary to focus the work, given how exponentially broader rustc is compared to polonius
  • the number of participants could be a factor, but I don't think so given how sprints are applied in similar fashion in "hack weeks" or game jams
  • there were some very concrete pieces of work to do, known beforehand, which mostly needed 1) execution (which was the point of clearing our calendars and having the time available to do so), or 2) discussion / group synchronisation to unblock specific topics and pieces of work

lqd (May 17 2021 at 16:34):

knowing what to do beforehand, or having a list of topics to pick from, was important (at least to me) in that it would not take away from the sprint time to figure these out

lqd (May 17 2021 at 16:40):

differentiating between the results-based tasks and activity-based tasks is tough for me on some of these, because the difference can be subtle for smaller tasks like "our goal is to complete this activity"

lqd (May 17 2021 at 16:43):

there was a mix of both I feel, but also a mix of concrete tasks that a given person was familiar with, and knew how to tackle; and others with more unknowns, more explorative, or requiring analysis

lqd (May 17 2021 at 16:46):

(of course nothing was set in stone, and some new unexpected things were discovered, and achieved during the sprints themselves :)

lqd (May 17 2021 at 16:50):

maybe another difference could be that the polonius sprints were a natural progression of the roadmap and regular, day to day work (albeit infrequent). I don't think the vast majority of participants in the shrinkmem sprint work on reducing rustc memory usage all the time, while for the WG the sprints were more "business as usual" -- and maybe in that context, having a list of tasks to pick from could be even more useful (and also arguably a big factor in how successful they were)
