Topic: RFC template

Mara (Mar 09 2022 at 18:05):

I think we need a new RFC template. Or maybe additional alternative templates, as alternatives to the one we have now.

The one we have now is structured with these "Guide-level explanation" and "Reference-level explanation" sections, which doesn't really work well for things that aren't new language features. In my RFCs, I've mostly ignored them and divided it up in different sections that work better for what I'm trying to get across, but it seems that most RFC authors try to force their rfc into this format even when it doesn't fit nicely.

I think the current template can sometimes form a bit of a barrier for someone to write an RFC, if they just want to explain their new library api idea for example, but now need to somehow find a way to split their explanation in these sections.

I was thinking to add a few different styles of templates. E.g. a "problem(s)" and "solution(s)" template, or a "goal(s)" and "plan" template, or a template that's more focussed on changing or removing something rather than adding something new.

Or alternatively to just simplify the current template, saying that everyone is free to pick their own structure, as long as the document addresses the motivation, alternatives, drawbacks, etc., and has enough detail.

@T-lang @T-libs-api @T-cargo @T-rustdoc thoughts?

  • Title/Summary/metadata
  • What problem are you proposing to solve? (formerly "motivation")
  • What's your proposed solution?
  • How would you explain the solution to an end user, if it were already available?
  • What are the downsides of your proposal?
  • What are some alternative solutions (including not solving the problem at all), and the tradeoffs between them?
  • Are there open questions that you expect to solve through experimentation after this proposal is accepted?

Nick Cameron (Mar 11 2022 at 10:50):

I think the whole structure of RFCs is wrong and we probably should rely more on initiatives and other structures to do open design. However, I think it is worth changing the template as an incremental change. Like Mara, I would much rather see a description of requirements rather than a prescription of the structure of the RFC. I think a hybrid approach is probably best, e.g., have a template which is the metadata at the top, a title (please lets add a title to RFCs!), a summary (which is missing at the moment and necessary IMO), a freeform area, and a FAQ (which might be initially empty and built up during discussion). We specify that the freeform part should motivate the change, describe them in detail from a user's perspective, and discuss implementation details as appropriate for the feature. For the latter, we should encourage linking out to preliminary implementations, a repo or initiative for design work, or whatever.
