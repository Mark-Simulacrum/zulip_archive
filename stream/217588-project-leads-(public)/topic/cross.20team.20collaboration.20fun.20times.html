nikomatsakis (Apr 07 2021 at 20:59):

Hey project leads! I want to get some feedback on an idea I've been working on. I call it the Cross Team Collaboration Fun Times, or CTCFT for short (continuing Rust's tradition of joyfully inscrutable acronyms). The idea is to have a meeting, held every 2 weeks, where we will:

  • To celebrate and raise awareness of what is happening in the project.
  • To track progress on important efforts and communicate requests between teams.
  • To give a forum for cross-team discussion and collaboration.

This is similar to, but also radically different from, the "leadership sync" meeting that we attempted before. Some key differences:

  • The agenda will be curated and selected in advance (I'm planning to do that role, but I'd love help);
  • We will not give status updates, but will focus on items that offer lessons/action-items for other teams, or where interesting discussion is needed;
  • Meetings will be recorded and publicly posted.
  • The meeting targets all active members of Rust teams and groups, but it will be an open meeting, with anyone welcome to observe (but you will have to request the zoom link, which gives a bit of a chance to orient folks).

I'm hoping to organize the first meeting for May 2nd (first Monday in May). I'd love feedback on the idea but also suggestions for initial agenda items.

One particular thing I'm trying to decide is what time of day -- I expect we'll have to alternate a bit to manage time zone access. (Also-- maybe monthly is better?)
