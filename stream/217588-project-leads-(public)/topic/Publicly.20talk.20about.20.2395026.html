Esteban Küber (Mar 25 2022 at 19:12):

I'm looking at the conversation at https://github.com/rust-lang/rust/pull/95026 and https://github.com/rust-lang/compiler-team/issues/493 and I feel that it'd be a good idea to publicize this as widely as possible. I see that this will likely go out in the release notes and blog, but I was thinking of tweeting this out now, ahead of the official communications:

PSA: The Rust team is considering increasing the minimum glibc required by some Tier 1 targets from 2.11 to 2.17.

One of the reasons is LLVM is expected to raise the toolchain baseline to GCC 7.1, which is not supported by crosstool-ng versions that support glibc 2.11.

For glibc, the minimum version is important for CI builds to have the desired compatibility in ELF symbol versions. RHEL 7 has glibc 2.17, while SLES 12 started with 2.19 and updated to 2.22 in SP2.

You can see more information on the rationale at https://github.com/rust-lang/compiler-team/issues/493

If you're a platform maintainer that might be affected, the announcement will likely be part of the next stable release's release notes, but the change will be made at a later release.
