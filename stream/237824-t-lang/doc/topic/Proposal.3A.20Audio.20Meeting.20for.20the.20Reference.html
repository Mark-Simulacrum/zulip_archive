isHavvy (Jan 25 2021 at 22:05):

My notes from the meeting:

Layout:

Move to memory model;
Identity holes in what is described;
Describe in terms of memory and not just types.
Type representation attributes should still be on the layout page.

Place operand: Good to use;

Grammar: Explicitly document the ambiguity of expressions;

Attributes:

They're the only documentation we have on it.
Figure out where they're allowed but useless.

Boolean:

Describe the behavior of operators on the type chapter; link to this behavior
on the operators page. There's a weird part where the operator traits are impled
in the library and not in the language.

Normative:

People really really want us to have even a single normative statement. Would
need the lang team to sign off on this. It's hard because it's hard to verify
that it's correct. Other languages references aren't always specified great
either.

Quality:

Ultimately, the want for normativeness seems to be a want for assurance of quality.
It's hard to state whether a page is missing something, but some pages are
obviously missing quality. Perhaps we can leave comments stating that we believe
they are of the standard for the Reference.
